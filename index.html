About Me

LIS 020.92 — ALI, M. L.

Md. Liton Ali is an Information Science researcher and PhD student at the University of Arizona, dedicated to modernizing information access and transforming library ecosystems. Starting from his university days, he has spent nearly a decade working across more than 15 library and information institutions in Bangladesh, bridging traditional information management with cutting-edge digital and AI-driven frameworks.

He built much of this foundation at the Islamic University of Technology (IUT), serving as Assistant Cataloguer and Liaison Officer for the International Affairs Office — deploying Koha-ILS, digitizing patron onboarding, streamlining cataloging workflows, and supporting international students through every stage of their integration.

He holds a Master of Arts in Information Science and Library Management from the University of Dhaka (CGPA 3.97/4.00), graduating with the Md. Shahidullah Memorial Gold Medal, the Dean's Award of Honour, and the Provost Award of Excellence. His doctoral research builds on this record, extending into health misinformation and knowledge management, and continuing the zero-budget digital library framework he leads for resource-constrained school and college libraries across Bangladesh — work recognized with the Best Paper Award at IIKM 2025.

Field Engagement

Field Projects

Toward Designing a Zero-Budget Digital Library Framework for School and College Libraries of Bangladesh

Most school and college libraries in Bangladesh lack digital services, including basic library websites — and the primary barrier is insufficient funding. This ongoing project designs a framework that enables libraries, especially in developing and least-developed countries, to build and maintain digital services at zero cost, using free resources and requiring only minimal technical skill.
